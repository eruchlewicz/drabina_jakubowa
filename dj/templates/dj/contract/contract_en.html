an agreement has been made as follows:
§ 1 The volunteer works gratuitously and does not charge salary for his work. The value of the volunteer's benefits does not constitute a donation to the Organizer according to the Civil Code and tax regulations.
§ 2 The volunteer with whom the contract has been signed is obliged to perform services according to the schedule (may be expressed verbally) agreed on with the camp coordinator. Any changes to the contract should be made in written form.
§ 3 The volunteer declares that he has never been punished for an offense against minors.
The range of the services provided by the volunteer
§ 4 The detailed range of services provided by the Volunteer determined by him individually with the Coordinator and may include the following:
1. Accompanying the disabled person.
2. Acquainting the disabled person with the facilities where the camp takes place.
3. Helping the disabled person with their daily activities - body toilet, satisfying physiological needs, getting dressed, going for a walk, having meals.
4. Accompanying and helping the disabled person to participate in classes according to the scheduled plan of the day.
5. Organizing the disabled person’s free time (making them active through fun, games, reading, walking).
6. Doing small purchases.
7. Identifying the spiritual and religious needs of the person.
8. Various works for the Organizer (including cleaning in the House and in the surrounding area, helping with maintenance work depending on the Volunteer’s skills).
9. Taking the night duty for the disabled.
Volunteer rights
§ 5 The volunteer has the right to obtain information about his rights and obligations.
1. The volunteer has the right to safe and hygienic conditions of performing his services.
2. The volunteer has the right to be informed about the risks to his health and security relating to the services performed as well as protection rules against threats.
3. The volunteer has the right to training and formation according to the program for the duration of his stay.
4. The volunteer has the right to receive support in crisis resulting from the services performed during his stay.
5. The volunteer has the right to receive a written certificate of his volunteer work, with the specification of their range.

The volunteer’s Obligations
§ 6 During his/her stay, the Volunteer is obliged to::
1. Comply with applicable laws and the Organizer’s internal regulations, in particular the Regulations of Jacob's Ladder, Standards and guidelines for the protection of minors in educational practice and pastoral care of the MDBO Religious Congregation of Orione Priests in Polish Province.
2. Wear an identification badge while working.
3. Respect the universal "patient rights" of the disabled person, respect his dignity and keep his personal data secret, especially the knowledge about his/ her intimate sphere.
4. Take care of the person entrusted - be responsible for his safety, appearance, hygiene and health.
5. Carry out the undertaken tasks conscientiously, with due diligence, respecting the dignity of all the people participating in the camp and cooperating with other volunteers and staff.
6. Take care of the property entrusted by the Organizer.
7. 7 Communicate any changes noticed in the patient's condition as soon as possible to the Nurse/Coordinator.
8. Take up daily tasks according to the work schedule and the plan of the day agreed on with the Coordinator.
9. In case of an unforeseen necessity to leave the camp, arrange it with the Coordinator.
10. Attend volunteer meetings. The Coordinator may partially or fully release the Volunteer from this obligation.
11. Control the places of activities of the disabled (the surrounding area, building, rooms) and roads leading to them, in particular check that their condition complies with the safety and hygiene laws and regulations.
12. Control the equipment and the condition of technical devices and installations.
13. Make an attempt to remove any deficiencies and defects noticed, remove them on his own if possible. Promptly report any noticed deficiencies and errors threatening the health and life of the disabled to the Coordinator or the Organizer.
14. Stop the classes immediately when the condition of the site, building, room, equipment, installations, material is a threat to the disabled, volunteers, employees or other people.
15. Provide first aid and the follow-up to the disabled person immediately.
16. 16 In case of an accident, notify the Organizer or the replacement person about the accident, counteract its negative effects and take other necessary measures.
17. While performing your tasks, cooperate with other volunteer branches of the Organizer for the benefit of the disabled.
The Organizer's obligations
§ 7 The Organizer is obliged to provide and familiarize the Volunteer with Regulations of Jacob's Ladder Camps included in this contract.
1. The organizer provides the Volunteer with free accommodation and meals for the duration of the camp.
2. The Organizer provides the Volunteer with an ID badge.
3. The Organizer is obliged to inform the Volunteer about the risk to health and security related to the services provided and about the rules of protection against threats.
4. The organizer provides the Volunteer with support in crisis situations resulting from the service performed during the camp.
5. The organizer is required to issue a certificate of the services performed by the Volunteer, at his request.
6. The organizer provides training for the Volunteers.
7. The Organizer is obliged to provide the Volunteer with an insurance against the consequences of accidents in accordance with applicable law regulations.

Termination of work on Voluntary basis
§ 8 Either party may terminate this Volunteer Agreement immediately, but we try to keep a 2-day notice period.
1. The Organizer may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect in case of non-performing or improper completion of basic obligations under this Agreement by the Volunteer and in particular:
1) For the breach of this Agreement or the Regulations of Jacob's Ladder Camps or other internal normative acts of “Centrum Księdza Orione” (Father Orione Center),
2) For the consumption of alcohol and drugs during the Camp,
3) For indecent behavior.
